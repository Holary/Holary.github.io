Prefae

This book is primarilg designed to be a secquel to my book Transactional Analysis in Psychoeherapy1,but has been plannedf so that it can be read ann understaod independently.

这本书主要是我的书《心理治疗的交互分析》的续集，但已计划好，以便能独立阅读和理解。

The theory necessary for the analysis and clear understanding of gaames has been summarized in [Part I][2].

在第一部分中，我们总结了分析和理解游戏所必需的理念。

[Part II][3] contains descriptions of the individual games.

第二部分包含了对单个游戏的描述。

[Part III][4] contains new clinical and theoretical material which,added to the old,makes it possible to understand to some extent what it means to be game-free.

第三部分包含新的临床和理论材料，添加到旧的内容中，让我们能够在一定程度上理解免费游戏的含义。

Those desiring further background are referred to the earlier volume.

想了解更多背景资料的读者请参阅前一卷。

The reader of both will note that in addition to the theoretical advances，there have been some minor changes in terminology and viewpoint based on further thinking and reading and new clinical material.

两者的读者都会注意到除了理论上的进步,根据进一步的思考和阅读以及新的临床资料，在术语和观点上有了一些细微的变化。

The need for this book was indicated by interested requests from students and lecture audiences for lists of games,or for further elaboration of games mentioned briefly as examples in a general exposition of the principles of transctional analysis.

学生和讲座听众对游戏列表的兴趣表明了对这本书的需求，或者在交易分析原则的一般性阐述中简要提到的例子中进一步阐述游戏。

Thanks are due in general to these students and audiences,and especially to the many patients who exposed to view,spotted or named new games;

感谢这些学生和观众，特别是对于许多暴露于视图，发现或命名新的患者游戏；

and in particular to Miss Barbara Rosenfeld for her many ideas about the art and meaning of listening;

尤其要感谢芭芭拉·罗森菲尔德小姐关于倾听的艺术和意义的许多观点；

and to Mr Melvin Boyce,Mr Josep Concannon,Dr Franklin Ernst,Dr Kenneth Everts,Dr Gordon Gritter,Mrs Frances Matson,and Dr Ray Poindexter,among others,for their independent discovery or confirmation of the significane of many games.

还有梅尔文·博伊斯先生，约瑟夫·康坎农先生，富兰克林·思斯特博士，肯尼斯·埃弗茨博士，戈登·格里特博士，弗兰西斯·马特森夫人，雷·波因德克斯特博士，因为他们的独立的发现或证实了许多游戏的重要性。

Mr Claude Steiner,formerly Research Directer of the San Francisco Social Psychiatry Seminars and presently in the Department of Psychology at the University of Michigan deserves special mention on two counts.

克劳德·斯泰纳先生，旧金山社会精神病学研讨会的前任研究主任，现在就职于密歇根大学心理学系，有两点值得特别提及。

He conducted the first experiments which confirmed many of the theoretical points at issue here,and as a result of these experiments he helped onsiderably in clarifying the nature of autonomy and of intimacy.

他进行了第一次实验，证实了这里争论的许多理论点，这些实验的结果是，他在澄清自主和亲密关系的本质方面帮助很大。

Thanks are also due to Miss Viola Litt,the Secretary-Treasurer of the Seminars,and to Mrs Mary N. Williams,my personal secretary,for their continued help,and to Anne Garrett for her assistance in reading the proof.

也要感谢研讨会司库秘书维奥拉·利特小姐，和玛丽·N·威廉姆斯夫人，感谢他们一直以来的帮助，还有安妮·加勒特，感谢她在阅读证据方面的帮助。
